# User ROI — What FocusFlow Returns to an ADHD Professional

## The Question

Is EGP 500/month worth it?

For a professional with ADHD, the answer requires understanding what ADHD is already costing them — in time, in income, and in opportunity.

---

## What ADHD Costs a Professional

Research on adult ADHD productivity consistently shows:

| Impact Area | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Time lost to executive dysfunction | 2–4 hours per working day (Barkley, 2011) |
| Task completion rate vs. neurotypical peers | 30–40% lower without support |
| Income gap | Adults with untreated ADHD earn 17–35% less than neurotypical peers (Lensing et al.) |
| Project abandonment rate | Significantly higher — partially completed work has zero value |
| Decision-making delays | Avoidance of complex decisions causes measurable project delays |

## The FocusFlow Recovery Calculation

### Scenario: Egyptian ADHD Consultant

**Assumptions:**
- Billing rate: EGP 500/hour (conservative)
- Working hours available: 8 hours/day, 20 days/month
- Hours lost to executive dysfunction without support: 2 hours/day

**Without FocusFlow:**
- Productive hours/month: 120 (of 160 available)
- Lost revenue opportunity: 40 hrs × EGP 500 = **EGP 20,000/month lost**

**With FocusFlow (conservative recovery of 50% of lost hours):**
- Additional productive hours/month: 20
- Additional revenue recovered: 20 hrs × EGP 500 = **EGP 10,000/month**

**FocusFlow Pro cost: EGP 500/month**

**Net return: EGP 9,500/month**
**ROI: 20x the subscription cost**

## The Honest Framing

FocusFlow does not cure ADHD. It does not guarantee productivity. What it does is remove the most damaging friction points — the blank-screen paralysis, the lost context, the forgotten follow-ups, the overwhelm spiral — so that the professional's underlying capability can show up more consistently.

Even a 20% improvement in task completion rates for an ADHD professional paying EGP 500/month for Pro represents a return that is orders of magnitude larger than the subscription cost.
