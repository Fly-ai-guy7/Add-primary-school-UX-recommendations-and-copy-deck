# Angel Investor Talking Points

A reference sheet for investor conversations — live meetings, panels, accelerator pitches. These are the answers to the questions you will reliably be asked.

---

## "What does FocusFlow actually do?"

> "It's an AI executive function system for people with ADHD. You speak or type anything on your mind — messy, unorganised, whatever — and it classifies everything into tasks, ideas, and reminders, shows you one thing at a time, works alongside you during focus sessions, and remembers everything you've ever told it. It does the organising so the user doesn't have to."

---

## "Why ADHD specifically?"

> "Because ADHD is the one group that every productivity tool has actively failed. All existing tools require you to be organised before you can use them — that's the exact capability ADHD impairs. It's a design flaw that's been baked into the category for 30 years. AI is the first technology that makes it economically viable to fix it."

---

## "Why Egypt? Why this market?"

> "Egypt has 5–10 million people with ADHD. There is not a single productivity tool in Arabic designed for them. EGP-priced tools don't exist in this category — the global competitors charge in USD, which prices out most of the Egyptian market. Egyptian data residency requirements and the National AI Strategy create a favourable environment for locally built AI products. And the Arabic-speaking market extends to 400 million people — Egypt is the beachhead."

---

## "Who are the competitors?"

> "Globally, tools like Notion, Todoist, and Motion exist — but none are designed for ADHD, and none work in Arabic. There's a small category of ADHD-specific tools — Goblin.tools, Focusmate — but again, English-only, USD-priced, and limited in scope. In the Arabic ADHD productivity market, the competition is zero. That's both the opportunity and the urgency."

---

## "What's the business model?"

> "Freemium SaaS. Free tier builds habit and trust. Pro tier is EGP 500 per month — that's the core revenue driver. Entrepreneur tier at EGP 1,250 per month for business-level features. Break-even at 155 paying users. LTV:CAC ratio target above 25:1 on community-led acquisition."

---

## "Why are you the right founder for this?"

> "I have ADHD. I've run a business with ADHD. I've tried every tool that exists and none of them worked — not because I wasn't trying, but because they weren't built for me. That's founder-market fit at its most direct. This is not a market I researched. It's a problem I live every day. And the product reflects that — the design decisions, the copy, the UX all come from a genuine understanding of what ADHD users need that you can't get from user research alone."

---

## "What's the funding ask?"

> "We're not raising a formal round at this stage. We're building relationships with investors who understand this market and might want to be part of it when the timing is right. The business is designed to reach profitability on organic growth — so a future raise is about acceleration, not survival."

---

## "What are the risks?"

> "Three honest ones. First, ADHD user churn — our audience is more likely to adopt and abandon tools. We've designed against this at every level, but it's a real challenge. Second, AI API cost volatility — we're dependent on inference costs staying manageable. We've mitigated this with a multi-model architecture. Third, larger competitor entry — a well-funded global player could add Arabic ADHD features. Our defence is the Memory Vault switching cost, community trust, and local moats. None of these are existential, but they're real."

---

## "What does success look like in 3 years?"

> "8,000 paying users. EGP 57M ARR. Operational in Egypt and the Gulf. A loyal community of ADHD professionals who attribute meaningful career improvement to FocusFlow. And a product that has earned the trust to expand into the full Arabic-speaking market — 400 million people who have never had a tool designed for the way their brains work."
