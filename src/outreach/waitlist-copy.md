## Problem Section

**Body:**
> Notion. Todoist. Trello. Asana. Every one of them requires you to set up your system, maintain your projects, and prioritise your tasks — before the tool does anything useful.
>
> That's the exact capability ADHD impairs most.
>
> So you try the tool. It doesn't stick. You try the next one. Repeat. And the cycle of adoption and abandonment becomes its own source of shame.
>

## Solution Section

**Headline:**
> FocusFlow doesn't ask you to organise.
> It organises for you.

**Feature blocks:**

**Brain Dump**
Just talk. Or type. Anything. In any order.
FocusFlow classifies, prioritises, and structures everything you say.

**One Task at a Time**
No more 40-item to-do lists. One task. The right one. Nothing else visible.

**AI Focus Partner**
Works alongside you during sessions. Checks in. Keeps you on track.
Like having someone in the room who's always on your side.

**Memory Vault**
"What did I decide about that client?"
"What was my idea from last Tuesday?"
Everything you've ever said to FocusFlow is searchable, forever.

**Overwhelm Detection**
When you've got too much going on, FocusFlow quietly reduces what you see to three things.
No alarm. No badge count. Just calm.
