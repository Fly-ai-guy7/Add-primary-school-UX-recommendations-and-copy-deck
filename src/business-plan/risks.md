# Risks and Mitigations

## Risk 1 — AI API Cost Volatility

**Risk:** GPT-5 and future AI inference costs are subject to change. A significant price increase from OpenAI could compress margins substantially.

**Mitigation:**
- Multi-model architecture designed from the start (GPT-5 as primary, Claude and Gemini as alternatives)
- Monitor per-user AI costs monthly and set usage thresholds
- Explore Egyptian government cloud subsidies available under the National AI Strategy
- Long-term: fine-tune smaller, cheaper models on FocusFlow-specific tasks

---

## Risk 2 — User Retention

**Risk:** ADHD users are, by definition, more likely than average to adopt and abandon tools. The very audience FocusFlow serves is at higher risk of churn.

**Mitigation:**
- Onboarding is the product — the first Brain Dump delivers value before any setup
- No penalty for absence — Context Recovery makes returning frictionless
- Community accountability reduces individual churn (users who are part of a community stay longer)
- Evening briefing creates a daily ritual that builds habit over time

---

## Risk 3 — Larger Competitor Entry

**Risk:** Notion, Google, or another well-funded competitor adds ADHD-specific features, neutralising FocusFlow's differentiation.

**Mitigation:**
- Memory Vault creates increasing switching costs the longer a user stays
- The Arabic-language, EGP-priced, Egypt-data-residency combination is not easily replicated by a global player
- Community trust and founder credibility cannot be acquired; they must be earned
- Speed to loyal user base is the priority — get deeply embedded before larger players notice the market

---

## Risk 4 — ADHD Diagnosis Stigma

**Risk:** In Egypt and the broader Arab world, ADHD remains underdiagnosed and in some communities stigmatised. Users may not self-identify as having ADHD.

**Mitigation:**
- Marketing does not require self-identification — "for people who think fast and struggle to slow down" is valid framing
- FocusFlow is useful for anyone with executive function challenges, not only formally diagnosed ADHD
- As diagnosis rates increase (driven by global awareness), the accessible market grows

---

## Risk 5 — Regulatory and Data Compliance

**Risk:** Egyptian data protection regulations, GDPR obligations for EU users, and changing AI regulations could impose compliance costs.

**Mitigation:**
- GDPR-compatible architecture is built into the MVP spec from the start
- Egyptian data residency options planned for enterprise tier
- Retain legal counsel with technology and data protection expertise before launch
- Monitor Egyptian National AI Strategy regulatory developments proactively

---

## Risk 6 — Founder Single Point of Failure

**Risk:** As a solo founder, Bruce McNamara is the single point of failure for product vision, engineering oversight, and business development.

**Mitigation:**
- First advisory hires prioritised in domains where founder expertise is thinnest
- Documented product spec and architecture (this PRD) reduces key-person dependency on product decisions
- First full-time engineering hire planned immediately post any seed funding
- Active engagement with accelerator networks for mentorship and accountability
