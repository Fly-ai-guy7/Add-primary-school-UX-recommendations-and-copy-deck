# The Problem

## ADHD Is an Executive Function Disorder — Not a Motivation Problem

ADHD (Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder) is a neurodevelopmental condition affecting the brain's executive function system. It is not laziness, lack of intelligence, or poor character. It is a neurological difference in how the brain manages:

- **Prioritisation** — identifying what matters most right now
- **Task initiation** — starting work even when the path is clear
- **Working memory** — holding context in mind while working
- **Time perception** — sensing deadlines and urgency accurately
- **Emotional regulation** — managing frustration, overwhelm, and rejection
- **Task completion** — following through to the end

These are precisely the skills that professional success requires most.

---

## The Productivity Tool Paradox

The global productivity software market has produced hundreds of tools — Notion, Todoist, Asana, Monday, ClickUp, Trello. All of them share a fundamental design flaw for ADHD users:

> **They require users to organise themselves before they can benefit.**

You must set up your workspace, create your projects, define your tags, prioritise your tasks, and maintain the system — before the tool does anything useful.

For neurotypical users, this is a minor friction cost. For ADHD users, it is a wall. The organisational overhead requires the exact capability their condition impairs. The result is a cycle of adoption, abandonment, and guilt that repeats indefinitely.
